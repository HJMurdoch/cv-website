Volunteering with Raleigh International

Raleigh International runs expeditions in Borneo, Costa Rica and Nicaragua and Tanzania for young volunteers to work on community and environmental projects combined with a tough adventure challenge. I spent 5 weeks in Borneo in 2015, building a gravity fed water system to supply a small village with running water. It was a great experience - the work was challenging but rewarding. I was able to interact with the indigenous population and I met and worked alongside a diverse range of interesting and talented young people.

Work Experience at Woodside Energy

I worked for two weeks in the robotics lab at Woodside Energy in Perth, WA. I started a manipulation task brought to Woodside by ABB which involved designing a robotic system to carry out a simple task to make HV switching safer. I also had the chance to programme the NASA robonaut Winston. My time at Woodside was a tipping point for me as it showed me how I could combine mechanical engineering with robotics and software to improve products and systems and contribute to society.

Gradutae in Counter Terrosism at [dstl]

I am currently a Graduate Engineer at the Defence Science and Technology Laboratory (Dstl) in Porton Down working in the Counter Terrorism and Security Division. I am part of a team working on the development of advanced armour systems. I assist in and run destructive and non-destructive trials and experiments to measure the performance of different materials and geometric configurations and use the results of these tests to innovate improved systems.

I have created Matlab models and Excel macros to process experimental data to assess the relative performance of systems and inform the next iteration of the development. I also create conceptual 3D CAD models to determine the optimal geometry, mass and other mechanical properties required.

To inform the design of future experiments I conduct broad literature reviews in areas related to civil defence systems, these range from the future of wearable technologies for first responders to developments in materials science.

My role has utilised and developed my engineering training and experience and has challenged and improved my communication, problem solving and teamwork skills.
